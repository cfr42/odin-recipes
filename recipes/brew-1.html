Brew I: Toil & Trouble

Requires three cooks. Prepare in an isolated cave over an open fire. Freeze in portions for up to three months.

Ingredients

  • Poisoned entrails
  • 1 toad, prepared under a cold stone for 31 days and night
  • 1 fillet of fenny snake
  • 1 newt's eye
  • 1 frog's toe
  • 1 bat's wool
  • 1 dog's tongue
  • 1 adder's fork
  • 1 blind-worm's sting
  • 1 lizard's let
  • 1 howlet's wing
  • 1 dragon scale
  • 1 wolf tooth
  • 1 witch's mummy
  • 1 salt-sea shark's maw and gulf
  • 1 hemlock root dug up at night
  • 1 goat's gall
  • Slips of yew to taste, gathered during a lunar eclipse
  • 1 tiger's chaudron
  • 1 baboon's blood

While some traditional recipes contain additional ingredients, the consensus among contemporary cooks deems them an unnecessary extravagance. Since they are no longer stocked by most supermarkets and excellent results may be obtained without, I do not include them here.

Instructions

  1. Prepare large cauldron, light fire and circle once.
  2. Add the entrails.
  3. Stir in the toad and bring cauldron back to boil.
  4. Keep fire burning until cauldron bubbles.
  5. Add the snake fillet, bring to boil and bake.
  6. Mix in the eye, toe, wool, tongue, fork, sting, leg and wing.
  7. If you are making the powerful-trouble variant, let boil and bubble as you would for a hell-broth.
  8. Keep fire burning until cauldron bubbles.
  9. Add the scale, tooth, mummy and hemlock.
  10. Stir in the gall and yew.
  11. Cook until the gruel is thick and slab, before adding the tiger's chaudron.
  12. Keep fire burning until cauldron bubbles.
  13. Add sufficient blood to cool.
  14. Sing round the cauldron, arranging cooks in a ring.
  15. Enchant until charmed.

Based on the three witches' recipe in Shakespeare's Macbeth
