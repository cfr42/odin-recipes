Brew III: George's Marvellous Medicine

Prepare 1 or more large medicine bottles by cleaning and drying thoroughly. Brown glass bottles with screw tops are ideal, but even clear stoppered bottles will do in a pinch.

Ingredients

  • Golden gloss hair shampoo
  • Toothpaste
  • Superfoam shaving soap
  • Vitamin enriched face cream
  • Scarlet nail varnish
  • Hair remover
  • Dishworth's famous dandruff cure
  • Brillident false teeth cleaner
  • Nevermore ponking deodorant spray
  • Liquid paraffin
  • Helga's hairset
  • Perfume: ‘Flowers of turnips’
  • Pink plaster powder
  • Lipsticks
  • Superwhite washing powder
  • Waxwell floor polish
  • Flea powder for dogs
  • Canary seed
  • Dark tan shoe polish
  • Gin
  • Curry powder
  • Mustard powder
  • ‘Extra hot’ chilli sauce
  • Black peppercorns
  • Horseradish sauce
  • Orange powder for foul chickens
  • Purple pills for hoarse horses
  • Thick yellowish liquid for cows
  • Red sheep dip
  • Green pig pills for swine sickness
  • Engine oil
  • Antifreeze
  • Grease
  • Dark brown gloss paint

Instructions

  1. Add the shampoo to a large, clean stockpot or saucepan1.
  2. Add the toothpaste, shaving soap, face cream, nail varnish, hair remover, dandruff cure, denture cleaner and deodorant.
  3. Fling in the liquid paraffin, hairset, perfume, plaster powder and lipsticks.
  4. Throw in the washing powder, floor polish, flea powder, canary seed and shoe polish, ensuring any noxious fumes clear before adding the next.
  5. Add the gin.
  6. Chuck in the curry and mustard powders, followed by the chilli sauce.
  7. Add the peppercorns and mix in the horseradish sauce.
  8. Allow to rest for a few minutes before adding the orange powder and purple pills.
  9. Pour in the thick yellowish liquid and sheep dip.
  10. Sprinkle liberally with the green pills.
  11. Add the engine oil, antifreeze and grease.
  12. Pour in sufficient paint to simulate grandma's brown medicine.
  13. Place pot, pan or cauldron onto the hob and bring to the boil, stirring continuously.
  14. Cook until the mixture is thick and the dry ingredients have dissolved. The medicine should be a monotonously hideous shade of brown.
  15. Remove from heat and allow to cool completely before bottling.

1If you do not have a suitable stockpot or saucepan, your cauldron should work fine. Just scrub it well and dry carefully. If yours is cast iron, re-oil and bake as usual to renew the seal.

Based on the Wikipedia entry for Ronald Dahl's George's Marvellous Medicine.
